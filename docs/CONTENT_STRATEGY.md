## What is AEO/GEO?

The way people find information has fundamentally changed. Search engines are no longer the only gatekeepers â€” AI answer engines are now the first touchpoint for millions of queries. Our content strategy needs to optimize for both.

### AEO â€” Answer Engine Optimization

AEO is the practice of making content **extractable and citable by AI answer engines** â€” ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude, and whatever comes next.

When someone asks an AI "how do I host an AI agent?", the AI assembles an answer from sources it trusts. AEO ensures Sparebox content is one of those sources.

**Key principle:** Write content that an AI can directly quote as a confident, authoritative answer.

### GEO â€” Generative Engine Optimization

GEO goes a step further: optimizing specifically for **citations in AI-generated answers**. When an AI generates a response, GEO ensures Sparebox is named, linked, or referenced as a source.

The difference:
- **AEO** = Your content gets used in answers
- **GEO** = Your brand gets credited in answers

We want both.

### Where Does Traditional SEO Fit?

Traditional SEO still matters â€” organic search traffic isn't going away. But the game has shifted:

| Era | Goal | Metric |
|-----|------|--------|
| SEO (2010s) | Rank #1 on Google | Search position |
| AEO (2020s) | Be the answer AI gives | AI citations |
| GEO (2025+) | Be the source AI credits | Brand mentions in AI responses |

**Our approach:** Write for humans first, structure for AI extraction, optimize for traditional search as a bonus. Good AEO content is naturally good SEO content.

## AEO Best Practices

Every piece of content we publish must follow these nine principles:

### 1. Answer-First Content (The "Snippet Trap")

**Lead every article with a direct, concise answer** to the primary question â€” in the first paragraph. This is the content AI engines extract.

> âŒ Bad: "In today's rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence..."
> âœ… Good: "The easiest way to host an AI agent is to use a P2P marketplace like Sparebox, starting at $10/month. For self-hosting, you need 8GB+ RAM and Docker."

**Rule:** If someone reads only the first paragraph, they should have a useful answer.

### 2. Question-Based H2/H3 Headings

Use natural language questions as section headings. These are the exact queries people type.

> âŒ Bad: "## Hosting Options"
> âœ… Good: "## What Are the Best Options for Hosting an AI Agent?"

**Why:** AI engines match headings to queries. A heading that IS the query dramatically increases citation likelihood.

### 3. Structured Data / Schema Markup

Every blog post must include appropriate JSON-LD schema markup:

- **Every post:** `Article` schema (headline, author, datePublished, publisher)
- **Posts with FAQ sections:** `FAQPage` schema (question/answer pairs)
- **Tutorial posts:** `HowTo` schema (steps, tools, estimated time)
- **Site-wide:** `Organization` schema for Sparebox

Schema markup is machine-readable. It's the clearest signal to AI engines about what our content says.

### 4. Tables and Comparison Charts

AI engines love structured, extractable data. Every relevant article should include at least one table or comparison chart.

**Use tables for:**
- Feature comparisons (Self-host vs Cloud vs P2P)
- Pricing breakdowns
- Hardware specs
- Pros/cons lists

**Format:** Clean markdown tables. Keep them concise â€” 3-6 rows is ideal. Label columns clearly.

### 5. Statistics and Citations

Include real numbers. Cite sources. AI engines trust authoritative, data-backed content.

- Quote specific prices, specs, and performance numbers
- Link to primary sources (documentation, research, benchmarks)
- Use recent data â€” freshness matters
- Attribute claims: "According to [source]..."

**Rule:** No vague claims. "Most users save money" â†’ "Hosts earn an average of $X/month on Sparebox."

### 6. Entity Consistency

Always refer to **"Sparebox"** â€” capitalized, one word. Never "Spare Box", "sparebox", or "SpareBox". Build entity recognition.

Consistently describe Sparebox as:
- "Sparebox, the P2P AI agent hosting marketplace"
- "Sparebox connects AI agents with reliable hardware from everyday hosts"

Repetition builds entity recognition in AI models. Be consistent across every piece of content.

### 7. Topical Authority

Don't write one article about AI hosting. **Write twenty.** Cover the entire topic cluster:

- Beginner guides â†’ intermediate tutorials â†’ advanced optimization
- Different angles: cost, performance, reliability, earnings
- Supporting content that links to pillar content

**AI engines recognize topical authority.** A site that covers a topic comprehensively gets cited more than one that has a single article.

### 8. Freshness

AI engines favor recent content. Keep articles updated:

- Review and update all posts quarterly
- Add "Last updated: [date]" to every post
- Refresh statistics and pricing annually
- Publish new content consistently (2-3x/week)

### 9. FAQ Sections

Every post ends with an FAQ section â€” 4-6 questions with concise answers. These are AEO gold.

**Why FAQs work:**
- Each Q&A is a standalone snippet an AI can extract
- FAQPage schema markup makes them machine-readable
- They target long-tail queries naturally
- They keep readers on the page longer (good for traditional SEO too)

**Format:** Use `<details>` or styled FAQ components. Keep answers to 2-3 sentences.
